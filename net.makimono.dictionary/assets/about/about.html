Acknowledgements

Makimono is based on the work from several other projects. Special thanks go to Jim Breen responsible for JMDict and Ulrich Apel who is responsible for Wadoku and KanjiVG.

JMDict

JMDict is a multilingual Japanese dictionary database created by James William Breen.

The JMDict content is used in the dictionary section of Makimono. All dictionary translations from Japanese to English / German / French / Russian are based on the JMDict.

© Electronic Dictionary Research and Development Group
© 1998-2007 Ulrich Apel (German translations)
License: Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 3.0
Website: www.edrdg.org

Hispadic

Hispadic is a Japanese / Spanish dictionary database.

The Hispadic content is used in the dictionary section of Makimono. All dictionary translations from Japanese to Spanish are based on it.

License: Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 3.0
Website: hispadic.byethost3.com

KANJIDIC2

The KANJIDIC2 is a XML-format kanji database.

The kanji section of Makimono uses the KANJIDIC2 content.

© Electronic Dictionary Research and Development Group
License: Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 3.0
Website: www.edrdg.org

KanjiVG

KanjiVG is a description of the sinographs (or kanji) used by the Japanese language.

The kanji stroke animation and drawing instructions in Makimono use the SVG paths from the KanjiVG project.

© 2009-2012 Ulrich Apel
License: Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 3.0
Website: kanjivg.tagaini.net

KRADFILE-U

KRADFILE-U is used for the multi radical kanji lookup and for the list of alternative radicals for each kanji entry.

© 2001 / 2007 / 2009: Michael Raine, Jim Breen, Electronic Dictionary Research and Development Group, Jim Rose, KanjiCafe.com
License: kanjicafe.com
